Masks for children

With the reopening of schools and colleges across the globe during the COVID-19 pandemic, the need for the proper use of masks has taken a place of utmost importance. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), as well as UNICEF and WHO, have decreed some important measures for children wearing masks. Age and ability play an important role in understanding who should and should not wear a mask.

Younger children, especially those under the age of five, should not be made to wear masks. Children under the age of five would not completely grasp the need for the mask in the first place. Parents could indeed send their child to school wearing a mask, only to have it exchanged, removed, or worn in a non-conducive manner, when in school itself.

So should children wear masks?

Masks are essential in one scenario- when social distancing is impossible. In this case, the parent is responsible for the mask being worn appropriately and ensuring that the proper mask size is used for the children. Additionally, for children between the ages of six and eleven, masks should become priority only when the area they live in has a high transmission of COVID-19. Children should be taught how to use the mask by themselves; however, there is no guarantee that they will use it effectively even when armed with that knowledge. A best practice is to make sure that adult supervision is always on-hand to assist the children.

When should children avoid wearing masks?

As children cannot be stopped from playing or doing any physical activities, it is important to remember that masks should not be worn when on the playground. Masks are inevitably hard to breathe through, and tend to compromise the breathing of the child- leading to its own set of problems.

In lieu of the lack of masks on the playground, parents should ensure that other measures are carried out. The number of children playing together should be limited. Maintaining social distancing is essential and has to be carried out to the largest degree. Hand hygiene is all-important- make sure children wash their hands after interacting with other people as well as surfaces.

The threat of COVID-19 does not keep the public from living out their regular lives- this includes children. Thus, it is essential to carry out whatever measures possible in order to maintain a safe and sanitary environment for them instead. Furthermore, it is important to educate and inform children of the importance of masks as well as how to effectively use them.

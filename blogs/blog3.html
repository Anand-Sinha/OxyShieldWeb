How to take care of your masks

How can I reuse my mask?

N95 masks are the best protection against corona virus in the current pandemic. They are essential when stepping out in public and act as one of the three main steps of protection against the virus (along with sanitizing surfaces and bodies, and social distancing). There are some rules and regulations when it comes to taking care and reusing your N95 mask.

The public has already been warned against touching their nose, eyes and mouth. In addition to this, you should also avoid touching your N95 mask. The outer area of the mask holds the concentration of dust and toxic particles. Thus, when removing your mask, you should only touch the rubber ear straps. Moreover, the mask should only be removed in a sanitized space, away from other people.

How can I reuse different types of masks?

Your simple, homemade, DIY mask can be washed in the same way as regular clothes. These masks do not offer excessive protection, but they are useful for quick use around the house. Since they are made with regular fabrics, they can be washed by hand and then left to hang in order to air-dry.

Surgical masks are not meant for reuse. These masks are usually meant for one-time use and should be thrown away. They can be salvaged if they have not been worn by an infected person. If their shape and structure remains, they can be disinfected if kept in a plastic bag along with desiccated gel to absorb the moisture from it. However, if these masks have any sign of dirt, blood, snot or other bodily fluids, it is a better practice to dispose of it. Even in disposing, the mask should first be folded in half, folded a second time and then rolled and thrown away- ensuring that the toxic particles are kept within the folds of the mask itself.

How do I safely sanitize my mask?

N95 masks can be reused up to two or three times. It is often recommended that you keep three to five masks at a time in order to maintain a rotation of masks when carrying out the process of disinfecting. N95 masks do not expire in terms of filtration- however, the bands that go over your ears can loosen or melt in the sun, affecting the airtight seal that needs to cover the mouth and nose.

Most people try to wash or disinfect the N95 masks. Experts recommend that, instead, the masks should be kept away in a sealed and sanitized space for three to five days- after which the virus is no longer viable. Disinfectants are not recommended as the wearer will end up breathing in the disinfectant itself when they use it next.

In the midst of a shortage of N95 masks, reusing your mask is a good practice. If you maintain proper hygiene and sanitization practices, the mask remains viable for at least two or three rounds of usage and help in the curbing of the pandemic.

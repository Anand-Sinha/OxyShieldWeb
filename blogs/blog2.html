What are the Best Practices for Masks during Covid-19?

Your mask acts as a protective barrier between you and the toxins in the air around you. It is essential to wear your mask- especially in public spaces- to ensure that you and your community are protected against the spread of not just Coronavirus, but any airborne toxins in your surroundings. The mask is a fairly simple piece of cloth; however, there are some dos and don'ts when it comes to handling and wearing the mask.

How do you make sure your mask is effective?

From the moment you buy your mask, you must ensure some things. The mask should form a tight seal around the nose and mouth. The masks that have ear loops are more useful when it comes to maintain hygiene as it prevents the wearer from touching the face of the mask, even accidentally. The fabric of the mask should allow for breathability, but should not have a valve. Lastly, the mask should be eligible to be washed in a washing machine or by hand in order to make sure you can clean it before reusing it.

Before putting on your mask, you should make sure your hands are clean. This practice repeats for when you take off your mask. The mask acts as a clean barrier between you and your surroundings; thus, you need to make sure it stays as sanitized as possible. The common cloth masks should not be used for more than two hours at a time, as by this time a layer of toxins builds up within its folds and can lead to the wearer breathing in the very toxins they are trying to avoid.

The most common mistake while putting on a mask is that people tend to leave the nose uncovered. Covering just the mouth is effective to protect others when you yourself are coughing. However, it does not protect you from outside particles infecting you and worsening your condition. Thus, you need to make sure the mask covers your nose and extends down below your chin, effectively covering the lower half of your face.

How do I eat in public with my mask on?

With parts of the world returning to normalcy, restaurants and cafes are entertaining customers with health guidelines in place. On your part, you can ensure that you keep your mask clean while you eat. With clean hands, remove your mask- only touching the ear loops- and keep it aside in a clean airtight bag. Alternatively, you can carry a second, clean mask to wear after you finish your meal. Avoid touching your nose, mouth and eyes at any point while putting on or taking off your mask. After you remove your mask, and even before you put it on, make sure you wash your hands. This is to ensure that your food is not contaminated nor do you end up with food particles on your mask.

It is also very important to make sure that your mask does not get wet. If your tongue touches the inside of your mask, it gets drenched with sweat, rain, or any kind of liquid, make sure you replace it immediately. Wet cloth allows particles to pass through the mask easily. Replace the wet mask with a clean one and wash it thoroughly or throw it away.

The mask is not the ultimate protection against Covid-19 or any other airborne diseases. You must make sure you wash your hands, maintain social distancing and wear the mask in order to create a safe and sanitary space around you.

Corona Virus versus Shopping

With lockdowns and its many restrictions, shopping for essentials becomes a challenge of its own. When making your trip to the market, it is essential to employ the basics of sanitary practices- maintain six feet social distancing, wearing a mask, and sanitizing surfaces along with your own hands. These rules apply even more when conducting oneself in a public space; however, there are some additional practices that help minimize the spread of the virus.

How do we sanitize during and after making a trip to the market?

The employees, as well as other customers have most likely touched the supermarket’s items in the duration of their working hours. Even the door- if not manned by a doorman or otherwise automated- becomes a hotspot for the spread of Covid-19. To minimize exposure risk, ensure that one is always carrying:

a) a sanitizer to use after opening the door and after exiting the shop and

b) an N95 mask that forms a seal around the nose and mouth.

These essential items help maintain a sanitized environment around your personal being. Additionally, one must maintain a six feet distance from other shoppers at all times. Avoid letting others handle your goods as much as possible. When it is unavoidable, make sure you sanitize your hands right after handling the goods. It is always better to thoroughly wash one’s hands to get rid of lingering toxins; however, this is not always possible when out-and-about. Thus, carrying the hand sanitizer is a vital step when making a trip to the market.

Another essential item to maintain sanitization is a disinfectant kept at home. This disinfectant should be accessible as soon as you enter your house. Spray your shopping bags with the disinfectant and then let the bags sit for a minimum of two hours. This helps minimize the risk of exposure to Covid-19 within the space of your own home when you return from a public space.

What are my Alternatives for shopping during Covid?

Shopping during Covid does not necessarily have to be done physically. Several stores have moved their operations online and encourage contact-less delivery. Groceries and other essential items are now available online. If you have to meet the delivery person, it is important to remember to wear a mask, thoroughly wash your hands after unloading the delivery at home, and disinfecting the packaging that your delivery comes in. None of the measures for basic sanitization are particularly complicated or difficult. Nonetheless, they are important to carry out in light of a global pandemic.

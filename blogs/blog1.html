When are Masks not needed?

When are Masks not needed?

There are several instances in which wearing a mask during the Covid-19 is not necessary or recommended. For children under the age of five, masks are not recommended unless under parental supervision. Those with health concerns- especially those with lung problems- as well as those carrying out physical activities, have to exercise caution when wearing a mask.

What health concerns entail not wearing masks?

For those with asthma, wearing masks is not the best practice. Those with mid or well-controlled asthma can wear masks for short periods. However, when the case is severe and the person is prone to heavy medication, exacerbations, and hospitalizations, masks can do more harm than good. In this case, social distancing and hand hygiene become your best friends.

For those with intellectual disabilities, mental health diseases, or any sensory sensitivities, masks can prove to be a challenge. Healthcare workers or guardians should be close and on-hand in order to ensure that the mask is easy to put on and remove. Again, social distancing and hand hygiene act as further deterrents when masks cannot play their full role.

When should we avoid wearing masks?

Masks are ineffective when they are wet. Thus, wearing a mask when it is raining or when you are at a swimming pool has no real purpose. Those who work in areas where the risk of heat-related illnesses is high- such as construction workers- may have to consult health professionals in order to understand which masks are the best to use in the situation. In case masks for outdoors workers are not available, social distancing and constant sanitization play an important role in maintaining safe and sanitary spaces.

There are few circumstances when masks need to be worn but are not convenient. For those who are deaf or hard of hearing would have a hard time communicating when masks are on. Alternatives in this case would include wearing a clear mask, or writing down what is needed to be said. Thus, some instances include finding alternatives for masks while maintaining both hand hygiene and social distancing.

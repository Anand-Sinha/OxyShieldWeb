Are DIY masks useful against Coronavirus?

Are DIY masks useful against Coronavirus?

In lieu of the global pandemic, many debates have arisen as to the use of homemade masks in the prevention of Coronavirus. The Covid-19 Virus is communicable by air. Thus, it is indeed essential to wear a mask when out-and-about in public. DIY masks can indeed be effective against the virus; however, there are limitations to this effectiveness.

So why use a homemade mask?

In a pinch, DIY masks can serve as a protective barrier against the airborne virus. Simple cotton cloth masks however, are useless against the virus. If one were to fashion a mask that consisted of several thick layers, and formed a tight seal around the mouth and nose area, then the efficacy of the mask would significantly improve. The mechanisms of masks are simple- first, they form a seal around the nose and mouth in order to prevent airborne toxins from infiltrating the respiratory system. Second, the thick layered cloth allows for a staggered barrier between the polluted air and the wearer. Essentially, micro-particles should be caught on the fabric that is worn, such that fewer particles of the virus reach the wearerâ€™s respiratory system.

Homemade masks are not ineffective against Covid-19- provided they are constructed well and adhere to the above-mentioned necessities. Keeping these two main criteria in mind, effective homemade masks can be made from densely woven and layered material in order to be somewhat effective.

DIY Masks versus N95 Masks?

While DIY masks can prove to be a layer of protection against the N95 Virus, it does NOT act as a replacement for the N95 masks. The main reason for this debate was the scarcity in the availability of the N95 masks especially when it came to the requirements of medical professionals. The N95 mask is an essential part of the protective gear worn by medical professionals. It is also the most effective in providing protection against Coronavirus. Where scarf material, cotton and polyester provide less than 10% protection from the virus, N95 masks can block out 95% of toxic particles for the wearers.

The most effective type of mask is indeed the N95 Valve-less mask. Nonetheless, DIY Masks are not completely ineffective if made in a manner that

a) protects the nose and mouth of the wearer by providing a tight seal and

b) blocks particles due to thick layering of materials.

So, can DIY masks substitute for N95 masks?

The answer is simple: no, they cannot. Instead, homemade masks can act as a baseline protection when an N95 mask is not available. However, in the face of a global pandemic, it is essential to maintain an N95 mask for yourself and your family in order to be able to properly protect yourself.
